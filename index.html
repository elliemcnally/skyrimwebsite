A Study of Skyrim by Bethesda: the Roles of Sounds and Images in the Game


In this site I will to explore the use of audio in Bethesda's SkyrimThe skyrim soundtrack, composed by Jermey Soule, creates an atmosphere unattainable without it.

Secunda

The first audio example I have chosen is Secunda, this piece plays exclusively at night while in the open world. This piece is one of the most well known and memorable pieces of the Skyrim soundtrack. This piece instantly takes the player back to where they were in the game when it first played. It encompases the feeling of wandering alone in the dark in an unknown land.

Caught Off Guard

The second example I have chosen is Caught Off Guard. When the player character is ambushed by an enemy, this track plays. This track is a stark contrast to the former. The prominent drums and horn transport the player to a battlefield, and it is up to them to make it out alive.

Game Play


Skyrim is an open world role-playing game developed by Bethesda Studios in 2011.

The story focuses on the player characters journey to defeat Alduin the World-Eater and help the inhabitants of the game world of Tamriel.The soundtrack is not only immersive, but nostagic for those having played the game. Every composition reminds the player of a notable moment or atmosphere, bringing fantasy to life.

The story of the game is also completly optional to complete. The player may choose to complete the main questline or simply wander the world as they please, the game ends when the player is done with the experience. As Skryim is an open-world roleplaying game, the player can choose their own adventure, sideing with whomever they choose.

Barthes' "The Reality Effect"

In The Reality Effect Barthes illustrates the importance of small, innocuous details. These details have no affect on how the game is played or how it is interpreted, they are simply there to complete an environment. Skyrim is full of these little details. NPCs will often speak throwaway lines, barrels in crypts will have fresh produce inside, even the loading screens give little anecdotes bearing no relevance to the gameplay itself.
